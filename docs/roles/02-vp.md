# Role: Vice President (`vp`)

> **Tier:** 1 — Executive  
> **RBAC Token:** `vp`  
> **Access Level:** Read-only across all operational domains · No write/sign authority  
> **Session Timeout:** Web: 30 minutes

---

## 1. Role Summary

The **Vice President** is the senior-most operational executive in a 503B outsourcing facility. In Clarix, the VP role is an **executive observer** — they have read access to every operational domain, a dedicated Executive Dashboard, and receive automated escalations for critical events. However, they do not perform clinical actions (no batch signing, no deviation closure, no inventory adjustment).

The VP's value in Clarix is strategic visibility: real-time KPIs, trend analytics, audit readiness scores, and the ability to drill into any batch, deviation, or EM excursion without leaving the platform.

In a 503B facility, the VP typically holds one of these combined titles:
- **VP of Operations** — responsible for production output, staffing, facility efficiency
- **VP of Quality** (or Chief Quality Officer / CQO) — responsible for quality systems, regulatory posture, and FDA relationships
- Some organizations have both; in Clarix, a single `vp` token covers both perspectives

## 2. Primary Responsibilities

### 2.1 Strategic Oversight
- Monitor facilities KPIs: daily batch throughput, on-time release rate, critical deviation count, EM excursion frequency
- Assess overall audit readiness score — a composite metric (0–100%) calculated from open deviations, overdue CAPAs, training compliance, and calibration status
- Review weekly/monthly batch yield trends and identify systemic quality issues
- Hold production and QA managers accountable for performance targets

### 2.2 Escalation Recipient
Clarix auto-escalates the following events to the VP role:
- Deviations open > 30 days without closure
- CAPA overdue past scheduled completion date
- Critical severity deviation (`severity = 'critical'`) opened
- Environmental action limit exceeded (EM excursion with no linked deviation)
- Batch release rate falling below target threshold

### 2.3 Regulatory & Business Intelligence
- Review audit-readiness reports before FDA inspections
- Review batch yield by product line for commercial planning
- Monitor training compliance percentage across all departments
- Assess inventory health and procurement risks (supply chain continuity)

### 2.4 Reporting & Decision Support
- Export analytics reports (batch yield, deviation Pareto, EM trending, training compliance)
- Compare period-over-period performance metrics
- Use drill-down dashboards to investigate specific batches, rooms, or products without needing to request reports from managers

## 3. Daily Workflow in Clarix

### Morning Review (10 minutes)
1. Open **Executive Dashboard** (`/dashboard`)
2. Review 4 KPI tiles: Batches Today, Open Critical Deviations, EM Status, Inventory Health %
3. Check **Audit Readiness Score** — if < 95%, review open items dragging it down
4. Review any escalation notifications from overnight

### Weekly Review
- Open `/reports` → select Batch Yield Trend (7-day)
- Open `/reports` → Deviation Pareto by product and root cause
- Open `/reports` → Training Compliance Matrix (who's overdue?)
- Brief with Production Manager and QA Manager based on data

### Monthly Review
- Full EM trending review — room-by-room viable counts over 30 days
- Vendor qualification review — any suppliers on hold or approaching requalification
- CAPA effectiveness review — are corrective actions actually working?

## 6. Restrictions

| What | Why |
|------|-----|
| No write access on any operational table | Executive view only — no ability to inadvertently alter records |
| No e-signature authority | Prevents executive override of QA decisions |
| No audit trail access | Audit log access restricted to admin + QA Manager (need-to-know) |
| No batch execution or release | Separation of duties — operational authority sits with PIC/QA |

## 8. Regulatory Context

In a 503B facility, the VP (or equivalent C-suite) is responsible for establishing a **Quality Culture** — the organizational commitment to compliance that underlies all GMP requirements.

Key regulatory touchpoints:
- **21 CFR §211.22:** The quality control unit must have authority — the VP ensures QA is adequately resourced and independent from production pressure
- **21 CFR §211.68:** Management must ensure computer systems are appropriately validated — VP is the business sponsor for Clarix as a validated system
- **FDA 483 Observations:** If the facility receives FDA 483 observations, VP is typically the executive who receives them and commits to CAPA timelines
- **Annual Product Reviews:** VP reviews APRs (Annual Product Reviews / PQRs) for strategic decision-making
